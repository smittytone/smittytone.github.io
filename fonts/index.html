Logo Typefaces

The fonts used in the Smittytone’s Software Stack banner

 

This site’s logo is an homage to some of the great typefaces of the increasingly distant past, ie. the 1960s, 1970s and 1980s.

For some background about why these typefaces in particular, please see this blog post.

There are currently nine typefaces used, selected at random and re-selected every 20 seconds. They are:

Baby Teeth

Designed by Milton Glaser in the early 60s after seeing a hand painted sign in Mexico City. Used on a large number of LP covers in the latter years of the decade. Baby Teeth is not available in digital form, so the version used here is a reconstruction built from from a Letraset catalogue.

Chromium

Designed by David Harris in 1983. Available from MyFonts.

Countdown

Designed by Colin Brignall in 1965, this typeface was regularly seen in 70s sci-fi TV, Space 1999 in particular. Available from MyFonts.

Data 70

Designed by Bob Newman in 1970, this was inspired by the likes of E-13B, which was created for cheque and credit card numbers. Used in lots of outer space shows. Available from MyFonts.

Marvin

Designed by Michael Chave in 1969, this was possibly the most widely used font on the covers of sci-fi novels in the 70s and 80s. The typeface used here is Marvin Visions, a version of Marvin, which is not available in digital form.

Pipeline

Designed by Michael Chave in 1970, this too was a sci-fi book cover favourite. The typeface used here is Elle, The typeface used here is Elle, designer Rian Hughes’ take on Pipeline, which is not available in digital form. Available from MyFonts.

Pluto Shadow

Made available by foundry Face Photosetting in 1969, this is another font not available in digital form. The version used here is a reproduction of it called K22 Lucifer No.1.

Quicksilver

Designed by Dean Morris in 1976, this is a particular favourite of mine. The version used here is an amateur’s reconstruction that I stumbled upon many years ago. Like Chromium this was a very popular typeface in the 80s.

Stop

Designed by Aldo Novarese in 1971, this has been a favourite of mine since seeing it used on a Cyberman T-shirt. Available from MyFonts.
